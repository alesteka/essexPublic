Course outline

In the following module we will examine the ethical responsibilities of Computer Science Professionals and will be able to understand relevant computing applications, hardware and software perspectives.

Through understanding of relevant theories and programming concepts, we will be able to cope with the real world scenarios. Overall purpose is to develop an ethos of proffesional development and be able to understand computer's architecture, cyber threats, social implications and recent inovations in Computer Science.

4 Key principles of Code of Conduct

  • You make IT for everyone: Collaborative work and knowledge sharing is essential. Inclusive cooperation regardless race, religion, or any other condition.
  • Show what you know, learn what you don't: Know your week points and have positive attitude toward obtaining new knowledge.
  • Respect the organisation or individual you work for: Adhere to work standars and proffesional work ethics.
  • Keep IT real. Keep IT professional. Pass IT on: Respect other colleagues and prevent disreputations. Seek for improvements and encourage others.

(The Chartered Institute for IT, N.D.)

Forms of cybercrime

  • MALWARE: Broader term of different malicious activities executed either remotely or on the computer itself
  • VIRUS: Typcialy resides on a computer and gets activated when host programm starts. It corrupts the system, data and other programs.
  • WORM: Main feature of latter malware is to replicate itself and spred over the network of computers, eventually degrades performance and lead to overload of entire network/internet.
  • TROJAN HORSE: This program enters into the computer's system as any normal programm, except it contains malicious software. Usually it get's trigerred when we start the programm/open the attachments.
  • SPYWARE: It is a software that tracks our computer's activities and collects information.
  • PHISING: In it's simple form it lures someone to enter/give confidential information. Usually through some application form.
  • DENIAL OF SERVICE: The purpose of latter attack is to overload one's system to the degree that becomes unresponsive.

(Brookshear et.al, 2019)

Logic gate

Gate is a device that outputs boolean value based on an input value. Today's Computers are constructed out of switching circuits, which serve as a communication mechanism between different components. To understand logical gates we can see that the circuits on the right picture are constructed out of a components called flip-flops. Latter device stores a single bit value until input changes. These building blocks can have different logic e.g AND, OR, XOR, NOT etc.

(Brookshear et.al, 2019)

Emerging trends

  • Scalability: With virtual machines replication of 'systems' have become simple.
  • Big Data: Refers to collecting and processing large amounts of data. It is crucial for companies to utilize new technologies to make better decisions.
  • Cloud Computing: Accessing computing resources over the internet whenever and wherever we want. Connection of multiple systems over the internet.
  • Data Mining: Collecting activity data over the internet. Moreover it is about processing patterns and relationships to solve business problems.
  • Artificial Intelligence: Machine that mimics human behaviour. Based on algorithms it can learn by itself and predicts the output.

    Types of network Topologies/Architectures:

  • Bus: All devices are connected to main cable. Data is transmitted in single line.
  • Ring: Every computer is connected to another computer's node, making a ring shape.
  • Star: Nodes are connected to common hub e.g Router.
  • Tree: Multiple nodes that form sub-hubs which are hierarchicaly connected to topmost hub (root node).
  • Mesh: Each device connected with each another device.
  • Hybrid: Combination of multiple aforementioned topologies.
  • Client-server topology: Network of computers that access resources on a server. It isolates clients from an administrator, therefore it is known as centralized architecture.
  • Peer-to-Peer topology: Simple network of computers. Each computer is able to share it's content within network.
